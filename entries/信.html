信
Uncategorized
Kun: makoto, shinjiru  |  On: shin
trust ・ faith ・ to believe ・ sincerity ・ sign ・ message ・ messenger

Explanation

This character combines the person element with speech. In early ritual script, speech depicts a formal oath to the deities, marked by placing a large tattooing needle upon a covenant vessel (sai) that held the written vow. Against this sacred backdrop, 信 names what people pledge to one another under divine sanction, hence the senses of truth and sincerity, and even the act of making something true. By extension it can mean a mark or sign, and it also serves for news or information and, by metonymy, the messenger who carries it.
