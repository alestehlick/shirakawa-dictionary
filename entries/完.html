完
Uncategorized
Kun: mattashi  |  On: kan
complete ・ perfect ・ to finish ・ to accomplish

Explanation

完 combines 宀, the roof of the ancestral mausoleum, with 元, a figure marked by an emphasized head. Shirakawa reads the graph within the context of ancestral rites: beneath the mausoleum roof, the highlighted head evokes head-centered rituals such as the genpuku coming‑of‑age, when the hair was tied up and a coronet donned. In this same ritual sphere, 完 depicts the formal report of a warrior’s safe return after battle, offered before the ancestors in thanks for survival. From the idea of bringing affairs back under the protective roof and concluding them without harm, the character takes on the meanings of completing, carrying through to the end, and being whole or perfect. Related graphs like 冠 (the coronet of adulthood) and 寇 (an enemy ritually subdued at the mausoleum) confirm 完 as a character rooted in mausoleum ceremonies.
