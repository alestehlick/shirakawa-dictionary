申
Uncategorized
Kun: mousu  |  On: shin
to state humbly ・ to report

Explanation

Shirakawa reads 申 as a pictograph of a lightning flash: a strong vertical shaft flanked by parallel streaks of light refracting to either side. Lightning was understood as a visible manifestation of divine power, so the graph originally bore the sense of “deity” and served as the ancestor of 神. Because a bolt shoots out obliquely and seems to lengthen, the graph was also linked to ideas of extending and piling up, and in later usage came to express the polite act of speaking or reporting. Once 申 took on this verbal sense, a new character for “deity,” 神, was formed by adding 示, the altar sign used in rites, to 申.
