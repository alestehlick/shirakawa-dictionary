妃
Uncategorized
Kun: kisaki  |  On: hi
consort ・ queen ・ princess

Explanation

妃 is a compound of 女 “woman” and 巳, the pictograph of a snake—a creature revered as a nature spirit. In early ritual language (祀), such spirits were formally worshiped, and oracle-bone divinations ask whether the “Spirit Consort” should be propitiated, showing 妃 used for a woman honored as a deity. In bronze inscriptions it appears in exalted posthumous titles together with 妣 “deceased mother,” as in formulas like “Royal Deceased Mother, Filial Great Consort,” where 妃 marks an honorific rank. From these sacred and honorific uses, the character came to denote the king’s consort—the wife of the Son of Heaven—hence the later sense of queen or imperial consort.
