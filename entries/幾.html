幾
Uncategorized
Kun: iku, ikutsu, ikura, ikubaku  |  On: ki
how many ・ how much ・ sign ・ omen ・ faint

Explanation

幾 is a compound that marries the thread element 丝 (the early form of the later 糸, representing a decorative thread) with 戈, a ritual pike. In ancient usage this was a halberd adorned with fixed streamers of thread, a weapon endowed with the power to ward off malign forces and used when probing suspicious or uncanny happenings. From watching for subtle hints and inquiring into them, the graph came to suggest faint indications and portents—signs that something is about to begin—and by extension the sense of ‘faint, dim.’ Its original nuance was that of critical scrutiny and questioning (as in the notion later expressed by the pairing of 譏 ‘to censure’ and 察 ‘to examine’). Over time the character also took on its familiar interrogative use, asking quantity in ‘how many’ or ‘how much.’
