玉
Uncategorized
Kun: tama  |  On: gyoku
jade ・ jewel ・ gem ・ precious stone

Explanation

A pictograph of three pieces of jade threaded on a cord, with early forms even showing the string passing through at the top and bottom, evoking pendant jades that hung from a leather girdle. Ancient people felt that lustrous, adamantine jade held a living energy or spirit, and they fastened it to the body to draw on that power. Jade was also central to funerary rites; the tomb of Queen Consort Fu Hao, wife of the Yin king Wu Ding some 3,300 years ago, yielded a great number of exquisitely worked jades. In Japanese the reading "tama" connects with "tamashii" (spirit), and the graph serves as a laudatory epithet in compounds such as "gyokuza" (the imperial throne).
