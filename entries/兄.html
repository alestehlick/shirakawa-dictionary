兄
Uncategorized
Kun: ani  |  On: kei, kyo
elder brother ・ older brother

Explanation

In the earliest forms, 兄 is a compound of 口 and a person. Here 口 is not a mouth but the sacred container that held written prayers and covenant texts. The figure shows a person in profile bearing this vessel on the head—an image of someone engaged in worship. Because within the family the eldest brother presided over ancestral rites, the graph naturally came to mean “elder brother.” Early variants even add sleeve ornaments used in ritual dance or depict a kneeling posture, underlining this liturgical role. When the altar sign 示 is added to 兄, it yields 祝, the ritual officiant (hafuri), further confirming the character’s origin in sacred service.
