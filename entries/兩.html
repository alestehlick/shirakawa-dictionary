兩
Uncategorized
Kun: futatsu, moro  |  On: ryou
two ・ both ・ pair

Explanation

Shirakawa reads 兩 as a pictograph of the yoke (eku) used to harness draft animals. In chariot teams two horses were hitched abreast, and the graph sketches the connection of their yokes, an image that naturally yields the senses of “two,” “both,” and things set side by side. From this base comes 輛, tied to vehicles in formation, while in 倆 the idea extends to the coordinated dexterity of the two hands.
