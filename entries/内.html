内
Uncategorized
Kun: uchi  |  On: nai, dai
inside ・ interior ・ inner

Explanation

This character began as a pictograph of an entryway: sturdy uprights set together with a roof above, marking the doorway of a building. By contrast, the graph for iru (入) shows an entrance without a roof. In bronze inscriptions, 内 often appears where one would expect 入, as in writing “entering the gate” with 内門, emphasizing the act of going within. From this sense of having passed through a doorway, the character came to mean the inside or interior, and by extension the inward realm of the heart and mind, as seen in words like naishin (one’s inner feelings) and naisei (introspection).
