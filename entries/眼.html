眼
Uncategorized
Kun: manako, me, miru  |  On: gan, gen
eye ・ eyeball ・ pupil

Explanation

眼 is a phono-semantic compound: the left side 目 shows the eye, while 艮 serves as the sound indicator for gan/gen and also carries an image Shirakawa links to ritual power. He illustrates this with the related graph 限, which depicts setting up an apotropaic eye before the gods’ ladder to heaven as a ward, a scene that makes intruders recoil and turn back. From this ritual backdrop, 眼 denotes the eye endowed with such potent, spellbinding force. In usage, manako names the central part of the eye, seen as the source of penetrating vision and the basis for expressions like ganriki or ganryoku, the power of sight.
