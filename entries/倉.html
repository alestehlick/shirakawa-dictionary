倉
Uncategorized
Kun: kura  |  On: so
storehouse ・ granary ・ storeroom

Explanation

Ancient forms of this character are pictographic, showing a roofed structure raised above the ground: an open space below that does not touch the earth, with a chamber above where goods are tightly packed. It depicts a high-floor granary designed to keep cereals dry, and by extension a storehouse or storeroom. From this idea developed related graphs, such as 鄙 (hi), which originally evoked farmland with grain stores and came to mean the rustic or rural district, and 廩 (rin), denoting a granary specifically for storing rice.
