誓
Uncategorized
Kun: chikau  |  On: sei
to swear ・ vow ・ oath

Explanation

This character is a picto-phonetic compound that joins 言, the spoken words of a vow, with 折, which originally depicts cutting plants or trees with an axe (斤). That cutting was a solemn ritual gesture performed when swearing before the deities, and in the compound 折 both evokes the act and lends the sound (sei, as in 逝). From this ritual setting the graph first meant “to swear to the gods,” and later broadened to the general senses “to swear” and “oath.” By extension it can also express the idea of being circumspect or refraining. Ancient rites also included snapping a consecrated arrow while making a vow; accordingly, 矢 could itself be read with the sense “to vow (chikau).”
