陵
Uncategorized
Kun: misasagi, oka, shinogu, koeru  |  On: ryo
mausoleum ・ imperial tomb ・ hill ・ to endure ・ to surpass

Explanation

A phono-semantic character: the right element marks the sound ryō and originally depicted a sacred structure for receiving the descending deity, with backward-pointing footprints indicating the god’s descent. In some bronze forms a 土 component—also the base of 社, “shrine”—is added to stress the idea of a place of worship. The left element is the hill radical, visualized by Shirakawa as a stepped ascent or “ladder of the gods” for moving between heaven and earth. Taken together, 陵 denotes a sacred elevation set where mountains meet the plain, a site prepared to welcome and venerate the divine spirit. From this it came to mean a hill, and, because imperial tombs were commonly established on such heights, a mausoleum (misasagi). Through graphic interchange with 凌, it is also used with the senses “to endure” and “to surpass.”
