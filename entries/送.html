送
Uncategorized
Kun: okuru, okuri  |  On: so
to send ・ to present (a gift) ・ to escort ・ to see off

Explanation

Shirakawa explains 送 as a phono-semantic graph in which the element 矣 serves as the phonetic and, in its archaic form, depicts both hands holding something out in offering. From this image the earliest sense was to bestow a present—hence to send or present something—and bronze inscriptions such as the Mao Gong Ding preserve this meaning in phrases of bestowal. The sense later broadened to sending people as well, for example dispatching attendants to escort a bride, as seen in 媵. Characters like 矣, 送, 佚, 媵, and 賸 thus form a lineage centered on the act of sending or presenting.
