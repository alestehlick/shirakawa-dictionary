堅
Uncategorized
Kun: katai  |  On: ken
hard ・ firm

Explanation

A phono-semantic character built on the graph 臤, which supplies the on reading ken. 臤 is formed by inserting the hand 又 into 臣, the eye turned upward with a large pupil that, in Shirakawa’s view, depicts one who serves the gods after the light of the eyes has been taken. The hand intruding into the eye conveys the act of injuring the pupil, evoking a mind under strain and a body drawn tight. Placed over the earth sign 土, this image is transferred to the ground itself—soil compacted and resistant—giving 堅 the sense of hardness and firmness.
