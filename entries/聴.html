聴
Uncategorized
Kun: kiku, yurusu  |  On: chou, tei
to listen ・ to hear ・ to permit ・ to grant

Explanation

Originally written 聽, this character is a compound built on the ear sign 耳, the graph 壬 depicting a person seen from the side standing on tiptoe in prayer, and a component of 德, evoking virtuous, keen receptivity. In oracle-bone and bronze forms, an ear is paired with a covenant vessel into which petitions to the gods were placed, picturing sung prayers and the capacity to receive the deities’ voices and revelations. 聴 thus names the sagacious faculty of hearing the divine voice and, by extension, means to listen and heed. From the notion of receptive hearing comes the sense of accepting another’s words or plea, hence permitting or granting, as in 聴許 “approval.” Related graphs illuminate the same ritual scene: 聖 portrays the worshiper who hears the gods, and 聡 (聰) expresses the keen understanding of that voice.
