参
Uncategorized
Kun: mairu, majiwaru, mitsu  |  On: san, shin
to participate ・ to visit ・ three ・ to gather

Explanation

Originally written 參, the character combines the image of hair gathered at the crown with three ornamental hairpins and a kneeling figure below. The upper part shows the three pins set into the chignon, while the added 彡 evokes the gleam of their beaded tips; below is a person seen from the side in a posture of worship. From the three hairpins it could mean “three,” from the unequal lengths of the pins it suggested unevenness, and from the gathered hair it developed the sense of assembling or joining—hence uses such as 参詣 for visiting a shrine and 参差 for irregularity. Because the simple numeral 三 is easy to tamper with by adding strokes, 参 came to be used in its place in writing to prevent alteration.
