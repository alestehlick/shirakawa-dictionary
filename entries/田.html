田
Uncategorized
Kun: ta  |  On: den
field ・ paddy field ・ to till ・ hunting

Explanation

A pictograph of a field divided into plots. In oracle-bone script a single plot is drawn as a rectangle, while in bronze inscriptions it settles into a neat square. Bronze evidence suggests 田 denoted roughly the area a single man could cultivate, hence its core sense of “field” and, by extension, “to till a paddy field.” In the oracle inscriptions, however, it often stands for “hunting, chase,” a usage still found in the Book of Odes and the Book of Documents; later, that hunting sense came to be written with 畋.
