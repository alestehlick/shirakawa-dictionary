問
Uncategorized
Kun: tou, toi, tazuneru  |  On: mon, ton
to ask ・ question ・ inquiry

Explanation

This character combines the shrine doors 門 with the square 口, which Shirakawa interprets not as a mouth but as sai, a ritual receptacle that held written covenants and prayers to the gods. Set before the double doors of a sacred enclosure, this vessel marks a formal act of petition: one stands at the gate to seek the deity’s will and receive a revealed answer. From this scene of inquiring after divine intent, the meaning naturally broadened to the general senses of asking, questioning, and making inquiries.
