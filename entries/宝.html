宝
Uncategorized
Kun: takara  |  On: hou
treasure ・ precious object ・ valuables

Explanation

Originally written 寶, this is a picto-phonetic character. It shows the roof of the ancestral mausoleum (宀), under which offerings are set out: jade (玉), cowry shells (貝, once used as valuables), and an earthen jar (缶) for wine or water. The jar element also serves as the phonetic, which underlies the Sino-Japanese reading. The graph thus names the precious items presented in the ancestral shrine—hence “treasure.” The Shuowen glosses it as “rare,” explaining it in the sense of a “rare treasure” (珍宝). Bronze inscriptions often include dedicatory phrases such as “to make a treasured ritual vessel,” reflecting its association with prized sacrificial objects.
