減
Uncategorized
Kun: heru, herasu  |  On: gen
to decrease ・ to reduce ・ to diminish ・ to cut down ・ to lessen

Explanation

減 combines the water element with 咸, here functioning as the phonetic base (kan) and carrying a ritual image. In Shirakawa’s view, 咸 depicts a sacred receptacle for covenant or prayer slips, sealed by placing the consecrated broadaxe 戊 upon it—an act that 'shuts in' and preserves the efficacy of the prayer. When the water element is added, it signifies watering down or sullying what is holy, thus diminishing its force. He evokes the same logic in 盜, where letting liquid drip into the blood of a sworn covenant profanes and breaks that bond. From this notion of diluting sacred potency, 減 came to mean to lessen, decrease, and cut down.
