考
Uncategorized
Kun: kangaeru  |  On: kou
to think ・ consider ・ examine ・ deceased father

Explanation

Shirakawa analyzes 考 as a phono-semantic graph. Its core shape matches the upper part of 老, picturing the long hair of an elderly person seen in profile; to this a phonetic element supplying the sound kō was added. In its earliest sense the character named the deceased father, paired with 妣 for the deceased mother; the binome 考妣 thus evokes parents who have passed away and, by association, the deep despondency of mourning. The familiar verbal senses—‘to think,’ ‘to compare,’ and ‘to examine’—developed later, following the kō reading and aligning with the sense of 校, and they came to dominate everyday usage.
