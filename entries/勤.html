勤
Uncategorized
Kun: tsutomeru, tsutomaru, isoshimu  |  On: kin, gon
diligence ・ to endeavor ・ to work hard ・ service

Explanation

A phono-semantic compound, 勤 sets 堇 as the sound marker for kin while drawing on its stark imagery: an ancient shaman, bearing a ritual covenant vessel for petitions on the head, hands crossed and bound, a figure tied to ceremonies seeking rain in famine. The accompanying 力 is taken as the outline of a plow (耒), the implement of cultivation. Together they evoke exertion in the fields to stave off hunger, and from this scene the character comes to mean to strive and apply oneself in service—working diligently, being fit for duty, and making sustained effort.
