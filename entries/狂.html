狂
Uncategorized
Kun: kuruu, kuruoshii  |  On: kyo
to go mad ・ crazy ・ wild

Explanation

Early forms of 狂 place the sign for a footstep (zhi, 之) upon the king’s axe (wang, 王), the axehead being the emblem of royal rank; 王 also serves as the phonetic that carries the on reading. When envoys departed on the king’s command, they trod upon the axe in a send‑off rite to receive its numinous power. From this image the character came to mean “to go mad” in the sense of being seized by extraordinary, otherworldly vigor—like the power imparted by touching the axe set before the jade throne. This positive coloring appears in the Analects’ pairing 狂狷: the “kuang” are those fired by lofty ambition who press forward. When perfectly balanced men were lacking, such inspired intensity was valued, and those who lived by high ideals apart from the common way were admired.
