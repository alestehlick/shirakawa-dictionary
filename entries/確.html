確
Uncategorized
Kun: tashika, tashikameru  |  On: kaku
certain ・ sure ・ firm ・ to confirm ・ to ascertain

Explanation

A phono-semantic character: the phonetic element read kaku shows a frame (冂) set over a bird about to take flight, conveying the idea of holding something back firmly. When this is combined with the semantic 石 “stone,” it adds the sense of hardness—something pressed, strengthened, or beaten solid with a stone becomes firm and steady. From this come the meanings “hard, firm,” and by extension “reliable, certain.” In Japanese it appears as tashika “certain” and the verb tashikameru “to make sure, to ascertain,” as in confirming the authenticity of something.
