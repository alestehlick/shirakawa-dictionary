情
Uncategorized
Kun: nasake  |  On: jo, sei
emotion ・ feeling ・ sympathy

Explanation

情 is a phono-semantic character: the heart radical 忄 indicates the domain of mind and feeling, while 青 provides the sound, pointing to the On reading sei (and by extension jo). The character expresses the movements of the heart when it responds to things—emotion as the mind’s active functioning. In Song learning (Xingli), 性 “nature” was treated as substance and yang, while 情 was its functioning and yin, the way nature manifests in response. The Book of Rites (Liyun) defines human feelings as seven innate responses—joy, anger, sorrow, fear, love, dislike, and desire—abilities “possessed without learning,” highlighting 情 as an inborn capacity. By extension it also came to mean sympathy and considerate regard for others.
