明
Uncategorized
Kun: akari, akarui, akarumu, akaramu, akiraka, akeru, aku, akuru, akasu  |  On: mei, myou
light ・ bright ・ clear ・ dawn ・ open ・ reveal

Explanation

Shirakawa reads 明 as a compound graph that originally set the window pictograph 囧 beside the moon 月. It pictures moonlight entering through a window—an image grounded in the semi‑subterranean houses of the loess regions of ancient northern China, where a shaft-like room with a single opening admitted light that was revered as the presence of the divine. From this, the character takes on senses of light and brightness, clarity, dawning or opening, and revealing; hence the compound 神明 (shinmei) denotes the gods.
