抵
Uncategorized
Kun:  |  On: tei
to resist ・ to oppose ・ to push against ・ to counterbalance

Explanation

Composed of the hand radical and the phonetic element 氐, this is a phono-semantic character that pictures exerting force with the hand. 氐, originally showing a handled knife trimming the bottom of something flat, contributes both sound and the sense of making a base even. Classical glosses already understood the word as pressing or pushing, and by extension recommending or advancing something. From the idea of applying the hand’s force to press on or push aside, the character comes to mean to resist, refuse, or go against. It also suggests two sides brought to an even level, which underlies uses like 抵当, where property stands as an equivalent counterbalance pledged against a debt.
