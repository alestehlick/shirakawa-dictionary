軟
Uncategorized
Kun: yawaraka, yawarakai  |  On: nan
soft ・ tender ・ pliant

Explanation

Shirakawa treats this as a phono-semantic character. Its original form was written 輭, with 耎 serving as the phonetic indicator and already carrying the senses of "soft" and "weak." He interprets 耎 as the front-facing figure of a shaman, hair cut short and without a topknot—a person who serves the gods—whose form here intimates pliancy. In established usage the on-reading settled as nan, and the form 軟 came to be used for "soft, tender, flexible" and by extension "weak."
