摂
Uncategorized
Kun: toru  |  On: setsu
to take in ・ to gather ・ to control ・ to assist ・ to support

Explanation

摂 is a phono-semantic character. Its original form 攝 sets the hand radical beside 聶, which supplies the sound and, in the Shuowen, is explained as “to speak softly into the ear,” suggesting an action done gently and at close range. Joined with the hand, the graph came to picture the careful drawing together of small, scattered things. This is seen in uses like 衣を摂る, “to arrange one’s clothing,” where a loose hem is lifted and set in order. From this core sense of gathering and setting right, the character developed meanings such as to take, to assist, to control, and to nurture or support.
