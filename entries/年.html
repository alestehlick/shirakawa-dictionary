年
Uncategorized
Kun: toshi  |  On: nen
year ・ harvest

Explanation

Shirakawa sees 年 as a compound of the rice plant sign 禾 and a person. In ancient ritual imagery, 禾 appears as a headdress symbolizing the indwelling rice spirit, while the human figure is a man performing a field dance at planting time to pray for abundance. From this scene arises the sense of minori, the ripened fruit or harvest, and because rice bears once per cycle, the graph naturally came to mean year. Oracle-bone inscriptions even record divinations phrased as “Will we be granted a harvest?” using 受年. He also notes the related form 委, depicting a woman in a lowered posture dancing gracefully with a rice-plant headdress. Man and woman together enact a prayer for a rich, auspicious year.
